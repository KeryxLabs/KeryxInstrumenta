# AVEC Foundations

## 1. What AVEC Is
AVEC is a semantic control geometry for language-native systems.

It is built around four operational dimensions:
- stability
- friction
- logic
- autonomy

AVEC is not a prompt style. It is an orientation model used to steer, track, and recover reasoning trajectories over time.

## 2. Why It Exists
The problem AVEC solves is continuity of meaningful behavior across sessions and tools.

The observed pattern:
1. Plain instructions can complete tasks but often drift in orientation.
2. Geometric orientation signals improve trajectory coherence.
3. Compression plus retrieval can re-instantiate usable orientation in later sessions.
4. Stored recovery cues reduce recurrence of known drift patterns.

This is the practical engine behind long-running orchestration workflows.

## 3. Geometrized OCEAN Framing
AVEC is a compact operational abstraction over human-language behavioral priors.

Working premise:
- Human text encodes psychometric and relational structure.
- Model training on human text internalizes those structures as latent geometry.
- Steering language can intentionally bias the active trajectory within that geometry.

This is why AVEC can be expressive and precise at the same time.

## 4. Expressive Register as Control Surface
Technical and colloquial registers can co-exist in the same high-rigor flow.

In AVEC terms, colloquial markers can shift relational stance, trust cadence, and engagement pressure without dropping analytical quality. That shift can improve attractor lock and reduce drift.

## 5. Meaning Retrieval vs Fact Retrieval
AVEC governs meaning retrieval.

Two retrieval planes:
1. Knowledge retrieval: external facts and references.
2. Meaning retrieval: orientation geometry for how reasoning should proceed.

These planes are complementary and should be logged separately in implementations.

## 6. Minimal Math Contract
Interpolation:

V_a = (1 - mu) * V_baseline + mu * V_seed

Where:
- V_seed = [stability, friction, logic, autonomy]
- V_baseline is neutral baseline, commonly [0.5, 0.5, 0.5, 0.5]
- mu in [0,1]

Aggregate integrity:

psi = sum(V_a)

psi is an integrity signal. Full vectors remain the authoritative state.

## 9. Dependency Direction
AVEC is upstream.

Implementations such as STTP and ACC consume AVEC semantics. They do not define AVEC.

## 10. Operational Sequence
1. Declare target AVEC geometry.
2. Execute work under explicit claim labeling.

5. Persist successful recovery cues for later retrieval.

## 11. Engineering Position
AVEC should be treated as an engineering substrate for semantic continuity.

It is strong where it is instrumented and measured. It remains open where deeper mechanism proof is pending. That combination is a feature, not a weakness.
